Turtle catergories

Hawksbill turtle

Hawksbill turtles, native to tropical waters, are characterized by their stunningly patterned shells and beak-like mouths. They play a crucial role in coral reef ecosystems as they feed on sponges, maintaining the health and diversity of coral reefs. Unfortunately, they face significant threats due to habitat loss and illegal trade.

Native places of Hawksbill turtles

  1. Rekawa Beach: Located in the southern part of the island.
  2. Kosgoda: Situated on the western coast.
  3. Pigeon Island National Park: Located off the coast of Nilaveli
  4. Mirissa: Famous for its picturesque beaches*.

Green Sea Turtle

Green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas) are magnificent marine creatures found in tropical and subtropical waters. They are known for their graceful swimming and herbivorous diet. These gentle giants can reach impressive sizes, with some individuals exceeding 1,500 pounds. Green sea turtles play a crucial role in maintaining healthy seagrass and coral reef ecosystems. Unfortunately, they face threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and illegal poaching. Conservation efforts aim to protect and preserve these endangered creatures for future generations.

Native places of green sea turtles

  1. Rekawa Beach: Located in the southern part of the island.
  2. Pigeon Island National Park: Located off the coast of Nilaveli

Leatherback sea turtle

The leatherback sea turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is an extraordinary species known for its unique features. It is the largest turtle, lacks a hard shell, and instead has a leathery, flexible carapace. Leatherbacks are found in oceans worldwide and undertake remarkable migrations. Unfortunately, they face threats such as habitat loss and accidental capture in fishing gear, making conservation efforts vital.

Native places of leatherback turtles

  1. Rekawa Beach: Located in the southern part of the island.
  2. Mirissa: Famous for its picturesque beaches*.

Olive Ridely turtle

The Olive ridley sea turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is a captivating species found in the warm waters of the Pacific and Indian Oceans. Known for its olive-colored carapace, this turtle is famous for its synchronized nesting behavior called arribadas. Thousands of females come ashore simultaneously to lay their eggs. Despite their remarkable nesting displays, Olive ridley sea turtles face numerous threats, including habitat degradation, pollution, and accidental capture in fishing gear. Conservation efforts aim to protect these endangered turtles and their nesting sites.

Native places of Olive ridely turtles

  1. Rekawa Beach: Located in the southern part of the island.
  2. Pigeon Island National Park: Located off the coast of Nilaveli
  3. Mirissa: Famous for its picturesque beaches*.

Loggerhead turtle

Loggerhead sea turtles (Caretta caretta) are known to frequent the coastal waters of Sri Lanka. While they may not be prominent nesting species in Sri Lanka, they can occasionally be encountered along the country's shores. These magnificent turtles face threats such as habitat degradation, pollution, and entanglement in fishing gear. Conservation measures are crucial to protect their population and ensure their survival.

Native places of Loggerhead turtles

  1. Rekawa Beach: Located in the southern part of the island.
  2. Mirissa: Famous for its picturesque beaches*.
